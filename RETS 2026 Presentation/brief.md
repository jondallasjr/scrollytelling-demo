# Original Brief — RETS 2026: AI Can Help You Sell More RIGHT NOW. Here's How.

## Project Goal & Audience

**Goal**: Deliver a 20-minute presentation that convinces Zimbabwe real estate agents AI isn't future tech — it's a tool they can use TODAY to sell more, faster. Land practical takeaways, not hype.

**Speaker**: Jon Dallas — AI developer who built Propertybook's SEO/EEAT content engine (450+ articles in 48 hours), Pam Golding's AI Chrome extension (80% time savings on listings), and 250+ workshops globally. TEDx speaker. Has lived and worked in Harare.

**Audience**: Zimbabwe real estate professionals — agents, agency owners, property managers. Mixed tech comfort levels. Many use WhatsApp and smartphones daily but haven't touched AI tools. Hungry for practical advantage in a competitive market.

**Event**: RETS 2026 (Real Estate Technology Summit), Zimbabwe. Jon's slot: 09:15–09:35 AM, right after the opening remarks. Second speaker of the day.

---

## Format & Technical Approach

**Format**: HTML presentation, single file, all CSS/JS inline. Keyboard/click navigation with progressive reveal (bullets appear one at a time). Works offline after load. Responsive — viewable on phones for attendees sharing slides later.

**Slide count**: 18–22 slides (roughly 1 slide per minute, some faster for impact).

**Navigation**: Arrow keys, spacebar, or click to advance. Slide counter visible. Touch-swipe for mobile.

---

## Design System

### Colors
- **Background**: Dark charcoal `#0F1419` (tech-forward, easy on projector)
- **Primary accent**: Electric teal `#00D4AA` (energy, innovation, stands out on dark)
- **Secondary accent**: Warm amber `#F5A623` (urgency, action, "RIGHT NOW" energy)
- **Text**: White `#FFFFFF` for headings, light gray `#B8C4CE` for body
- **Danger/caution**: Soft red `#E74C3C` (for cautionary tales section)

### Typography
- **Display/Headlines**: Montserrat Bold (700) — strong, modern, clean on projector
- **Body/Bullets**: Inter Regular (400) / Medium (500)
- **Stats/Numbers**: Montserrat Bold at large size — numbers should punch
- **ALL CAPS**: Section labels, slide category indicators

### Layout Principles
- Image-left, content-right for most content slides (per presentation style guide)
- Title/section divider slides: full-bleed background image with text overlay + dark scrim
- Stats slides: big number centered, context below
- Keep text minimal — this is a spoken presentation, slides support the speaker

## Performance Edition — 3-Act Structure

### Format

This is not a slide deck. It is a **performance runtime** — a single HTML file where each "moment" (keypress/click) can be:
- A static slide
- A live interactive widget (AI chat, Facebook ad generator, org chart)
- An image comparison / before-after reveal
- A video player moment
- A two-pane negotiation chat

The speaker advances manually. No auto-timing. The screen IS the character's computer.

---

### Act 1 — The Performance (~8 min)
*Jon plays "Agent Jon" — a real estate agent who has gleefully automated everything and lost the plot.*

**Segment 1: Opener — "Best Year of My Life"**
Agent Jon in full character. Big boasting stats: 37 properties sold, $4.2M in commissions. No mention of AI yet.

**Segment 2: The Secret**
"I'll let you in on a little secret. I have another agent." Beat. "An AI agent." (Let the wordplay land without over-explaining.)

**Segment 3: AI Listing Copy Demo**
- Fake AI chat interface (PropIQ-style, dark theme)
- Property details pre-filled: 4-bed Borrowdale, pool, double garage
- Keypress → AI "types" a full listing description in real time (typewriter animation)
- Counter shows: "Time saved: 36 minutes"
- Jon: "I've never been to this house. I don't know what it smells like. But the copy is beautiful."

**Segment 4: Image Enhancement Demo**
Four escalating beats, each a keypress:
1. Rusty satellite dish on roof → gone. "Standard stuff."
2. Abandoned car in driveway → gone. "Okay."
3. Construction scaffolding on adjacent plot → gone. "Hmm."
4. Neighbor's house blocking the view → gone. Open sky appears. [Pause. Let it land.]
5. (Optional) Overcast sky → replaced with blue sky. "I'll stop there."

**Segment 5: Targeting Ads Demo**
"But I'm not just listing. I'm finding buyers. AI doesn't wait for leads. It hunts."

Three signals play out in sequence — each more uncomfortable:
1. **Death** — Probate filing detected. "Estate of Margaret Ncube." Ad generates: "We know this is a difficult time..."
2. **Divorce** — Divorce proceedings detected. "Joint property registered both names." Ad: "Moving forward? Your space, your terms."
3. **Missed mortgage** — Pre-foreclosure stage 1. "Before the bank gets involved — sell on your terms."

Between each: "No law against it. In fact... it's great customer service."

**Segment 6: Agency Conversion Arc**
"I got so good at this, I thought: why am I still working for someone else? I started my own agency. The Jon Dallas Group."

Animated org chart — Jon at top, 5 staff below. Keypress by keypress, each converts:
- Emily (Listings) → AI Writer ✓
- Marcus (Social) → AI Content ✓
- Sarah (Leads) → AI Chatbot ✓
- David (Analysis) → AI AVM ✓
- Priya (Operations) → AI Operations ✓

Each conversion: cost counter updates. Gleeful energy throughout.

**Segment 7: The Firing**
"Then I did the math."
- 5 employees: $4,000/month
- AI tools: $47/month
- [Button: SEND TERMINATION EMAILS]
- Progress bar. "5/5 sent."
- "The emails were very empathetic. AI wrote those too."

**Segment 8: Full AI Operation**
Solo montage. Everything is AI now:
- Social posts: AI (every day, never typed a caption)
- Morning briefing: AI podcast (NotebookLM, plays in the car)
- Market valuations: AI
- Virtual tours: AI-generated walkthrough videos
- "I haven't written a sentence about real estate since February."

**Segment 9: AI-to-AI Negotiation**
"I stopped meeting clients."
- Phone-unlock lockbox: buyer lets themselves in, no agent needed
- One day: buyer showed up with their own AI agent
- Split-screen chat: PropIQ Agent vs HomeSense AI
- Messages appear one at a time — offer, counter, accept, contract, done
- Final message from Jon: "great, thx 👍 *(Sent from iPhone — Jon was at the gym)*"

**Segment 10: The Peak**
Minimal slide. Big text.
> "I am the future of real estate."

Long pause. Dead air. Then, quietly:
> "...Is this okay?"

No advance for several seconds. Let it sit.

### Act 3 — The Rules (~5 min)

**Segment 19: "So what do we agree to?"**
Frame: Zimbabwe gets to write its version of this story. These are starting points, not commandments.

**Segments 20–23: Discussion Cards**
1. **Disclose** — If AI touched it, say so. Audiences can tell. Trust is the only thing that can't be automated.
2. **Don't target grief** — The 5 Ds are real. Using them is legal. We can choose not to.
3. **Know what you're selling** — AI wrote the listing. You still have to answer questions about the house.
4. **Build the data now** — Every digital listing is training data. The agents who list today have a head start tomorrow.

### Design Notes for Performance Edition

- **Act 1**: Same dark charcoal `#0F1419`, teal `#00D4AA` accent — but *louder*. Agent Jon's screen should feel slick, impressive, slightly too polished.
- **Act 2 (Drop character)**: Hard cut to a lighter background — `#F5F5F0` or similar warm white — to signal the shift. Completely different energy.
- **Act 3**: Back to dark, but calmer. No animations. Typography only.
- Widget frames (listing demo, targeting ad, org chart) should look like real software UIs — not presentation slides. They ARE the demos.
- Slide counter: show as `[segment / 24]` so audience knows where they are in the arc.
