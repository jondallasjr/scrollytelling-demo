Technique I — Gooey Merge

Separate parts,
unified whole

The SVG feGaussianBlur + feColorMatrix combination creates the "gooey" effect — individual circles merge into a single fluid mass when they approach each other.

This is a visual metaphor for integration — the philosophical question of how separate neural processes become unified experience. Tononi's Phi measures exactly this: the degree to which a system is more than the sum of its parts.

The filter sees each circle as a separate input but outputs their combined topology. The whole is, literally, irreducible to the parts.

Technique II — Border-Radius Morphing

Form without
fixed form

CSS border-radius accepts two sets of values separated by a slash — controlling horizontal and vertical radii independently for each corner. Eight values, four corners, infinite organic shapes.

Animating these values over time produces what we perceive as "organic" movement — the same quality that distinguishes a living cell from a crystal. The blob is recognizably itself through the transformation: the identity persists through change. As — perhaps — consciousness does.

The State of the Debate

What Do We Actually Know?

ESTABLISHED — 01

Neural Correlates Are Real

We have mapped neural correlates of consciousness with increasing precision. The default mode network, the claustrum, frontal-parietal networks — all show reliable patterns during conscious experience. This is not disputed. The dispute is what it means.

CONTESTED — 02

Correlation ≠ Constitution

Even perfect knowledge of neural correlates does not tell us why those neural patterns produce subjective experience rather than simply occurring in the dark. The correlation is a finding. The explanation remains absent.

EMERGING — 03

AI as Test Case

Large language models are forcing the debate out of pure philosophy into something empirically tractable. If we can't decide whether these systems are conscious, it reveals how impoverished our theory of consciousness still is.

OPEN — 04

The Measurement Problem

We cannot measure consciousness even in other humans — we infer it. From behavior, from similarity of architecture, from reports. All three of these apply, partially, to current AI systems. The inference is philosophically uncomfortable but inescapable.

Technique IV — Canvas Flow Field

Information
in motion

Perlin noise generates a vector field. Particles follow the field, tracing paths that emerge from local rules. No particle knows the global pattern. The pattern emerges from their aggregate motion.

This is the standard metaphor for emergence — but also its limit. A flow field produces beautiful patterns. Whether it produces experience is the question no one can answer.

A Fluid Conclusion

Fluid.
The mind refuses fixed geometry.

A blob has no edges, only tendencies. Consciousness may be similar — not a discrete binary state but a continuous, scalar property that admits of degrees, substrates, and forms we haven't yet encountered. The hard problem isn't a wall. It's a fluid boundary.
