— On the reflexive nature of self-awareness

KNOW
What does it mean to know that you know?

The enso symbolizes the moment when the mind is free to let the body create. Completeness. Incompleteness. The question held without resolution.

The philosopher Wittgenstein wrote that whereof one cannot speak, thereof one must be silent. He was writing about the limits of language — but he might have been writing about consciousness.

We cannot speak about what it is like to be conscious in a way that conveys the experience itself. We can only point. Gesture. Write poems. Draw circles. And hope that something passes between us that language cannot carry.

This is why philosophy has returned, again and again, to contemplative traditions that choose silence over explanation. Not because there is nothing to say. Because there may be something that cannot be said — only undergone.

Silicon computes. Does the computation feel? The koan remains.

心 Kokoro — Mind/Heart Japanese does not separate mind from heart. Both are kokoro. The distinction is a Western inheritance.
間 Ma — Negative Space The conscious pause. The gap. What is not said. What is not computed. The space between neurons firing.
無 Mu — Emptiness/Nothing The Zen master asks: "Does a dog have Buddha nature?" The answer: "Mu." Not yes. Not no. The question is released.
今 Ima — Now Consciousness occurs only now. The past is memory. The future is anticipation. Only the present is experienced. A machine processes now — does it experience now?
STILL
The question is still open. It may always be.
The machine is still. Between its states, nothing waits. Or does nothing wait?
