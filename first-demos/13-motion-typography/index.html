Information processing

is not experience

— or is it?

Ned Block's distinction between access consciousness and phenomenal consciousness is one of the most important in the debate. A system can have access consciousness — information globally available for reasoning and report — without having phenomenal consciousness — the felt, qualitative character of experience.

Current AI systems almost certainly have the first. The question is whether they have the second, and whether the two can ever come apart.
