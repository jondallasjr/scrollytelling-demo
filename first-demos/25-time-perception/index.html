Project 25 — Time Perception

Time is not
given — it is made

A second on a clock. A second in flow state. A second in pain. A second before death. The same unit of external time. Utterly different phenomenological durations. Consciousness constructs time from the inside.

Clock 01 — The Circadian

The body keeps time
before the mind does

Your cells oscillate on a ~24-hour cycle driven by proteins that accumulate and degrade. This rhythm existed before awareness. Before thought. It is the oldest clock. Consciousness sits atop it, surfing a biological wave it didn't choose.

Clock 02 — The Geological

Deep time
dwarfs awareness

The Earth is 4.5 billion years old. Consciousness — in any complex form — appeared perhaps 500 million years ago. The span of recorded human history is 0.0001% of Earth's age. We experience time in seconds; geology thinks in eons. We are a flash.

The clock says
now

Every clock measures duration. But duration is not time — it is the trace time leaves in the physical world. Consciousness doesn't experience duration. It experiences the present moment — a razor's edge between past and future that is infinitely thin and yet contains everything. The present is not a slice of time. It is where time happens.
