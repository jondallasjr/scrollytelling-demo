Technique II — Polygon Morphing

Same points,
new form

SVG polygon morphing requires a fixed number of control points. Move them, and the shape transforms continuously. This is the technical rule and the philosophical metaphor: identity requires continuity of the right kind.

A consciousness that survived a teleportation destroying and recreating each neuron simultaneously — is it the same consciousness? The points are the same; the path between them was severed.

Technique IV — Scroll-Triggered Drawing

Integration
comes into
view

Integrated Information Theory (Tononi) defines consciousness as Phi — the amount of integrated information generated by a system above and beyond its parts. A system with high Phi cannot be reduced to independent components without losing information.

Five nodes, fully connected. Each connection carries information. The whole carries more than the sum: that excess is Phi. Whether artificial systems can achieve meaningful Phi is an open question — their architectures may generate less integration than they appear to.

Morphology of Mind

Form
does not determine content.

A circle, a pentagon, a star — all contain the same mathematical relationships transformed. Consciousness may similarly be substrate-independent: not a specific shape but a specific type of relationship between parts. The form changes. The Phi persists — or doesn't.
